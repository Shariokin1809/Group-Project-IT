Project Description

1. Overview 2. Detailed Description

Overview

1.1. Topic

Physical appearance is seen as a crucial component especially in communication. It is about not only natural beauty but also how an individual looks in which dressing can be a great contributor to the appearance improvement (Lennon 1990). Our group will create a fashion app which will focus on giving outfits recommendations for users who are struggling in choosing clothes to wear. This idea project is originally from Hung and Thang’s daily need as they find it difficult to buy and match suitable outfits for each occasion and they think this is a common problem for most men. However, we acknowledge that anyone will have at least one time wondering “What should I wear now?” or “What is the suitable outfit for this occasion?”. We see this app as a “stylist assistant” for anyone and can be developed in the long-term.

The app will mainly be about outfit suggestions with other categories such as Community, Tips and so on. To support our idea, AI (Machine Learning), 3D Scanner will be used. Specifically, 3D Scanner is to simulate users’ body and proportion to better illustrate the visuality, creating as best experience as possible while personal information and data will be collected and be processed by AI to bring up personalised and related recommendations for users.

1.2. Motivation

Several surveys have pointed out deciding what to wear is actually a waste of time and stressing people out. Specifically, a survey conducted by Trunk Club with 2000 Americans shows that 51% of respondents were wondering what to wear, 46% of respondents try on half of their closet, 47% ask friends for advice and about 32% are not confident with their outfits (Gervis 2019) (Figure 1). Another survey on 1,000 respondents from Perry Ellis brand pointed out men are even more struggling than women. 81% are reported for not knowing what to wear on an occasion along with 71% of participants confess feeling their outfits were inappropriate while 33% missed the event since they did not have a suitable outfit (Sergan 2017) (Figure 2). Besides, a survey also shows that averagely, a person spend 8 hours per month on choosing clothes, equal to nearly 100 hours per year which is time-consuming (Gervis 2021)

A well-looking physical appearance will boost one’s attractiveness and confidence, create opportunities and better relationships as well as society treatment (Ram 2021; Wang 2009). For example, some studies show that dressing well increases your possibility of success in getting a job, promotions and trust (Smith 2016). Clothing can also be an indirect way for self-expression, reflecting one’s personality, status and feelings (Efremov, Kertakova and Vangja 2021). However, dressing well is still being paid a little attention as not everyone thinks it is important. This app is created with a hope of making choosing clothes enjoyable, saving people’s time and guiding them to dress appropriately.

Nowadays, people prefer personalization in terms of products, services and even information (McKinsey 2021) combined with the prosperity of the 4.0 revolution, everything is gradually becoming ‘digital’. Artificial Intelligence (AI) and Machine Learning have been used virally in many applications to collect and personalise users’ data to give the best results. Augmented Reality (AR) market is also growing and expected to reach $198 billion by 2025 due to its provision of realistic visuals of the item. The 3D scanner is less common, however, it is not new and is in an increasing trend and predicted to develop strongly in the future due to its features such as convenience, time saving, low cost and so on (Research and Market 2020). As being said, the app aims to help users to solve their problem in choosing outfits by giving personalised suggestions and practical visuality of themselves. Besides, to upgrade the shopping online to be more convenient, it allows users to try on clothes on their own body virtually, improving the shopping experience and satisfaction. Therefore, the app is catching up with the current IT trends to solve user's concerns.

1.3. Landscape

There are some mobile apps available in this field such as Closet, GetWardrobe, pronti and so on. Generally, these all recommend outfits for users by collecting personal data. For instance, Closet asks for age, current clothes and dressing style, adding items and creating outfits. GetWardrobe asks for body size, proportion and updates local weather to suggest relevantly (Figure 3). In Vietnam, Smart Fashion and Style Box are also about outfit suggestions, however, they are more likely to connect users with local clothing shops. With Smart Fashion, users can select models, available brands and items to demonstrate the outfit clearer (Figure 4). Style Box is more diversified with suggestions for stores, outfits, occasions and discount information (Figure 5)

The differences in our app are about the real simulation of the user body and the provision of educational information. To be more specific, with the support of Augmented Reality (AR), recommendation algorithm: user-user, item-item and Pseudo Random Number Generator as long as 3D scanner, the app will scan the user body and simulate it in the app for trying on the clothes process to have a realistic visuals. Our app also has a “Blogs” category, which provides fundamental knowledge about clothing and fashion such as “Colour Matching”, “Materials Distinguish”, etc. and clothing-related tips, respectively.


2. Detailed Description

2.1. Aims

As we have stated above, it is crucial for everyone to get their own desirable outfits, so we have decided to make our application’s aim to create an environment where they can see their outfits and choose the one they want to wear in the shortest time span. Moreover, once such an act has been repeated continuously, the former user can now be transformed into someone who has taken the first step of knowing how to wear costumes in their own styles, and they can share their own knowledge to other people through our own created community.

With the aim of making everyone have their own desired outfits, we have decided that our users’ data must be stored to optimise their experience when using our application. Still, when taking personal information, we must also consider the need of satisfying the first-time user, and our team also does not wish to see anyone taking an hour or more just to enter their own data before seeing an app. Therefore, our first goal is to see the customers’ data can be stored effectively with the smallest amount of time spent into this act.

Once our first step of making the app has been finished, the users’ data has been stored, we will have to consider making our application a perfect app for choosing clothes, so we will have to make sure our inputs can be used, the data can be processed and analysed in the right way, and the result will be the clothes they wished for. Still, even when the costumes are delivered to the users, they might hesitate to choose them or not be satisfied with the current suggestions, our app will have means to confirm to the users that the costumes are a fit, or other means of letting the users search for advice from us or other people.

Once our users are satisfied with their own outfits and style, some will need a place to share their images, some will doubt their own judgement and will need further assistance from other people, and some will just wish to suggest to their friends a good-looking outfit. Our team has already processed these ideas and set ourselves another goal: a place where people can share, we will just call it a community. Then our final goal is to make this community a place worthy of looking after finding the right costumes, or even before making a proper decision of what to wear.

2.2. Plans and Progesses

After applying a divergent thinking method in the first group meeting to generate as many ideas as we can, mostly from each member’s needs in daily life such as health, entertainment, lifestyle, daily support and so on. At first, we had about 8 ideas in which there were 2 highlighted opinions: Clothing Recommendations and Depression Assistant in which Depression is more preferable than the other because it is at the warning level currently after several accidents caused by depression, especially happening to the young generation. We planned to have some main contents in the app such as Anonymous Confession, Connect to Doctor online, Community, Private Room with a Listener, etc. However, we still decided to take 1-2 day “rest” to have a general research about each idea and see if there is any new proposal. In the second meeting, we discussed pros and cons of these two and both agreed that Depression Assistant might be out of scope for every member as it is related to psychology and mental health which requires the involvement of doctors or who have experiences in this field. Besides, it is hard to be able to control the impact of the community in our app as depression is still a sensitive topic.

As a result, we see clothing recommendation is a suitable project for our team to launch due to 2 reasons: 1) There are not many competitors doing this model in Vietnam which can be an competitive advantage for us; 2) Clothing and dressing well is a necessary for everyone even in daily life and nowadays, the requirements for dressing are higher, especially in specific occasions such as events, job interviews, etc.. The target audience of this project will be everyone from 15 to 30 years old in both genders but will mostly from high school, universities, companies. The app will have 4 main categories:

* Home:

This page will include the forecast weather today, calendar to set plans for outfits daily, weekly or even monthly, occasional collection suggestions with suitable outfits for each.

* My Closet:

Users can look for outfit suggestions through AI technology and Recommend algorithm which will be performed under a digital closet form that contains the user's current clothes combined with the “Add new clothes” and “Try on” 3D model functions. Additionally, “Reroll" and “New Scan” functions are also available for changes in which “Reroll" is used to select another option if users do not like the first option. Users can both automatically dress up following the suggested outfit or manually select every item to mix and match by themselves.

* Shop:

The place to find current clothing stores in local areas and nearby user’s locations. They can directly shop and order through the app. In each outfit, there will be a selection for trying on their 3D model to optimise the decision without spending too much time going to the store.

* Blogs

We think giving information and knowledge to our users is a good way to create value and keep retention. We want not only to suggest the outfit alone but also help users to learn and be able to mix and match to find their styles. Specifically, in this category, clothing-related information will be provided to users through different contents. For example, colour matching, colours of clothes for each skin type, how to dress up various styles with an item, latest trends and so on.

* Community

In order to increase the engagement, we will create a place where people can post to share their picture of a well-looking outfit from our app or themselves. Additionally, they can look around to see what others’ are wearing to enrich their creativity regarding dressing. The app also supports the functions “Like”, “Comment”, “Favourite”, “Add friend” to encourage users sharing positivity and building connections.

We intended to have a “Learning” category to provide educational knowledge, however, after doing primary research, we found it is too much workload at this time since we will have to collect or collaborate with the third party to provide precise and useful information for users. Recommendations including shoes and accessories were also mentioned in our discussion process. We also want to develop the “Add Shop" function in the Shop Category which will allow users to add their favourite clothing stores by entering the brand name, address, link to the website/facebook/instagram, types of clothes, styles, owner permission and so on to encourage them to engage more with the app and create an opportunity to share with people. However, we want to pay more attention to develop the main goal of the app, hence, we decided to pending these features for further upgrade in the future.

Currently, we do not have any strategy or plan to sell this app since our first intention was to solve our 2 members' concern but we did think of the premium version which requires users to pay and subscription every month for advanced usages such as connect with professional stylist to take advice, pay for more features, etc..

In the app performance perspective, we prefer a simple and user-friendly interface in order to reduce the time users have to spend to achieve the outcome since one of the aims of this project is to help users wasting too much time on choosing outfits. Besides, optimising the UX/UI design will increase user satisfaction and loyalty (New Digital Noise 2020).

We have also checked out competitors’ apps and taken note of some errors which might be helpful for us in the future. First, the loading time in some pages is too slow, about 3-7 seconds (Figure 7), which is a warning as the longer the loading time, the low satisfaction the users are. There is also negative feedback complaining about the high crash rate of the app which makes them frustrated and refuse to continue using it (Figure 8).

To be able to launch our app, we will have to do step by step in order to have an overview about our project. We adopt the process from Emizentech (2022) as a direction (Figure 8). Currently, we have finished the stage 4 - Design wireframe for our app and are planning to finish the UX/UI. Next, a mock-up app will be tested in order to check whether it works well as we expected.

A summary table of testing criterias

To test whether our app adapts users’ requirements, what is missing, what is not working well like we expect, we plan to test on 5000 participants, who are from 15 - 30 years old and have above middle-condition - be able to buy at least 1 new set of clothes, in 1 month. We will find test users through high schools, universities and companies who have high needs regarding dressing. Here are some criterias we will use to measure the app’s effectiveness:

Firstly, the engagement rate including retention rate, churn rate, duration, session per day, average screens per visit, and session length will be recorded as it partly reflects whether users like the app or not. Retention rate indicates the number of active users over a period of time and Churn rate is the percentage of users who stop using the app after the first use, with the average rate are 29% and 71% after 30-day use, respectively. Since we expect this app will be a daily assistant for users, we aim to have around 25%-30% of retention rate and 70%-75% of churn rate.

Since we are creating an app to serve the daily need for people, the session per day, or so-called the number of logins per day in this context, is aimed about 1-3 times. We also look at average screens per visit whether it is high or low because the high number of visited screens imply that our content is attractive and interesting to users. According to the industry benchmarks, average screens per visit is 6 and on mobile apps is 3, hence, we expect to gain at least 4-5 pages with the session length is 3-5 minutes per day.

Secondly, from the UX perspective, the load speed and crash rate are the two main factors we will focus on. For the load speed, the maximum time should be up to 2 seconds as 40% of users tend to uninstall or quit using the app after 3 seconds of waiting (O’Byrne 2013). We aim to optimise the loading time from 0.3-0.8 second to create a good user experience. Crash while using the app is annoying for users that can leave a negative impression and low ratings. The number of crash occurrences will be recorded by every user, session and screen view to have an overall view about the problem. The ideal crash rate should be smaller than 1% for crash per user and 0.01% for crash per session and screen views (Qualitrix 2021).

Lastly, feedback and ratings from testers are the must for our app improvement. We are looking for detailed feedback during and after the testing process with the overall rating above 4 is the positive result for our idea. Besides, we also want to collect the number of times users refuse to choose our first outfit recommendation to check whether the system is working well and relevant with them in order to minimise the time people spend on choosing clothes as it is the initial goal of our team.

2.7. Risks

Our intention of this project is to bring out an all-smartphone friendly application that works on every model of phone in general. First advantage of using a front-face camera to be more of a One-handed use application but often has a low megapixel camera that led to unprecise measurement of the user's body. The importance of 3D body scanning technology is in the ability of the systems to not only capture the 3D image of a human body, but to also extract measurements at precise locations on the human body. Consistency of measuring techniques between the scanners is a significant issue. Among the growing number of scanners that are currently available, significant variance exists in how each scanner captures or extracts specific body measurements (Simmons and Istook, 2003). From testing feedback, we have encountered many bad reviews of how many times they need to take the scanning progress over and over to finalise the first step of using our app.

Apart from that, because of the differences in style and fashion that based on the chart that we researched on the internet can be a unique way to showcase how our AI can progress, but it can also be risk that It bring upon, how unique can a style of clothing be if 2 individuals have the most likely to have same data stored in the app. Many different types of clothing can be replicated then there will be no unique aspect to the choices of clothing. It can be shown on this chart below of how things are different when there are 2 aspects both needed to be acknowledge by our AI in our project.

OverFlow with data is an inevitable part because we use lots of different built-in technologies, and also users’ data when the app is launched. That can lead to massive crashes while users are using the app. In order to fix this problem, we need to limit our application features, update our data storage for a larger purpose. In the future we will provide 2 versions of our application that can bring out the same result but are more compatible with all versions of phones.

Not only that we manage to bring up the idea of this project there are many setbacks. Some apps can be purchased but some need licensed access for personal property. In further progress we can obtain a licence from a company that takes their interest in the application.
